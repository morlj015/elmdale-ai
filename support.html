🤖 Basics

What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence explained in a way that actually makes sense.

4 minute read

The simple version

Artificial Intelligence, or AI, is software that is designed to do things that would normally require human intelligence. That includes reading text, understanding questions, recognising images, making decisions, and generating responses that sound natural.

If you have ever asked Siri a question, used Google to auto-complete a sentence, or seen a spam filter catch an email before it reached your inbox, you have already used AI.

How is AI different from regular software?

Traditional software follows fixed instructions. You write rules, and the program follows them exactly. If you did not write a rule for a situation, the program does not know what to do.

AI is different. Instead of following rigid rules, AI learns from examples. You show it millions of emails and label which ones are spam. Over time, it figures out the patterns on its own and starts making accurate predictions on emails it has never seen before.

📧

Real example: A spam filter trained on 10 million emails learns that phrases like "click here to claim your prize" almost always appear in unwanted messages. It does not need a human to write that rule. It discovered it from the data.

Is AI new?

The concept goes back to the 1950s, but the AI you hear about today is genuinely different from earlier versions. Three things changed everything: far more data to learn from, much cheaper computing power, and new mathematical techniques that made AI dramatically more capable.

The AI tools available right now are the most powerful that have ever existed, and they are improving quickly.

What AI can and cannot do

It is worth being honest here. AI is remarkable at certain tasks and surprisingly poor at others.

  • Good at: reading and writing text, summarising information, answering questions, recognising patterns, translating languages, generating ideas
  • Less reliable at: maths that requires precise calculation, tasks that need real-time information it was not trained on, anything requiring physical actions in the world
✅

The key insight is that AI is best used as a force multiplier for knowledge work. Anything that involves reading, writing, categorising, or responding, AI can usually do faster and at much greater scale than a human.

What is an LLM?

Large Language Models are the engine behind tools like ChatGPT. Here is what they actually are.

5 minute read

Starting with language

An LLM stands for Large Language Model. The "language" part tells you its specialty: it is designed to understand and produce human language. The "large" part tells you how it was built: by processing an enormous amount of text.

We are talking about hundreds of billions of words from books, websites, academic papers, code repositories, and much more. By studying all of that text, the model develops a surprisingly deep understanding of how language works.

How does it actually work?

At a very simplified level, an LLM learns to predict what word is likely to come next in a sentence. If you write "The cat sat on the…" the model has read enough text to know that words like "mat", "floor", or "chair" are plausible continuations. It assigns probabilities to thousands of possible next words and picks the most appropriate one in context.

Do this over and over, word by word, and you get coherent, fluent, contextually appropriate text. That is the core mechanism behind every modern AI writing tool.

💡

The model is not "thinking" in the way humans do. It is performing extraordinarily sophisticated pattern matching. But the output can be so accurate and useful that, for practical purposes, the distinction often does not matter.

What can you use an LLM for?

Because LLMs understand language so well, they are useful in any situation involving text. Businesses use them for:

  • Drafting and editing emails, proposals, and reports
  • Summarising long documents into short briefings
  • Answering customer questions using information from your own knowledge base
  • Translating content into other languages
  • Classifying incoming requests so they reach the right person automatically
  • Writing first drafts of social media posts, blog articles, and marketing copy

What LLMs are not

It is worth clearing up a few common misconceptions:

  • LLMs are not connected to the internet by default. They know what they were trained on, but they do not automatically browse websites for new information.
  • LLMs are not always right. They can produce confident-sounding answers that are factually incorrect. This is called "hallucination" and it is something to be aware of, especially for tasks that require precise facts.
  • LLMs are not a replacement for human judgement in high-stakes decisions. They are tools that augment human capability, not remove it.

What is an Automation?

Automations are the pipes that connect your tools together. Here is how they work.

5 minute read

The core idea

An automation is simply a set of instructions that runs by itself, without you having to do anything. Something happens (called a trigger), and then a series of steps runs automatically (called actions).

You have probably used automations without realising it. When you pay a bill online and receive a receipt in your email seconds later, that is an automation. When a calendar reminder pops up before your meeting, that is an automation. The idea is not new. What is new is how accessible and powerful these tools have become for small and medium businesses.

⚙️

Simple example: A new form is submitted on your website (trigger) and the details are automatically added to your CRM, a welcome email is sent to the new contact, and your team gets a notification in Slack (actions). All without anyone lifting a finger.

What makes AI automations different?

Traditional automations are great for moving data between systems, but they can only follow the exact rules you set. If something unusual happens, they get stuck.

AI automations add a layer of intelligence. Instead of just moving data, they can read it, understand it, and make decisions based on what it says. They can write a personalised reply, summarise a document, categorise a request, or flag something that looks unusual, all as part of a fully automated workflow.

The building blocks

  • Trigger: The event that starts the automation. Examples include receiving an email, a form submission, a new row in a spreadsheet, or a set time of day.
  • Action: What happens as a result. Examples include sending an email, creating a record, generating a document, or sending a message in Slack.
  • Condition: Optional rules that make the automation smarter. For example, only send the email if the lead is from the UK.
  • AI Step: A point in the workflow where an LLM reads or generates text. This is what turns a basic automation into an intelligent one.

The tools that make it possible

You do not need to build any of this from scratch. Platforms like Make.com, Zapier, and n8n provide a visual canvas where workflows are built by connecting blocks together, much like snapping pieces of a puzzle. These platforms handle all of the technical complexity behind the scenes.

✅

Most businesses already use the apps that automations connect. Gmail, Google Sheets, HubSpot, Slack, Notion, Airtable. The automation just makes them talk to each other automatically.

The Business Case for Automation

Why businesses that automate early consistently outperform those that do not.

6 minute read

The hidden cost of manual work

Most businesses have a set of repetitive tasks that consume significant time every week. Data entry, copy-pasting between tools, chasing invoices, sending follow-up emails, formatting reports, onboarding new clients. Individually, these tasks seem small. Together, they often add up to tens of hours per week across a team.

The real cost is not just the time. Every hour a skilled person spends on routine admin is an hour they are not spending on the work that actually requires their expertise. That is the real loss.

Time savings

The most immediate benefit. Tasks that used to take hours now happen in seconds. A workflow that required a team member to manually process 50 new leads every morning, checking each one against your CRM, drafting personalised emails, and updating a tracker, can be fully automated to run overnight without anyone's involvement.

Cost reduction

Automation does not replace people. It frees them from work that does not require a person. This means you can grow your operation without proportionally growing your headcount. A business that automates its client onboarding process does not need to hire an extra person every time their client numbers double.

Fewer mistakes

Humans make mistakes, especially on repetitive tasks. Copying data from one system to another, typos, missed steps, and forgotten follow-ups are all natural consequences of doing the same thing hundreds of times. Automations do the same step the same way every single time. For compliance-sensitive or financially important workflows, this consistency is extremely valuable.

Scalability without growing pains

A well-built automation scales effortlessly. Whether it processes 10 requests a day or 10,000, the workflow performs identically. For growing businesses, this is transformative. You can take on significantly more work without significantly more overhead.

💡

The businesses winning right now are not the ones with the most staff. They are the ones that have figured out how to do more with the same team by removing friction from their processes.

Competitive advantage

While your competitors are still sending manual follow-up emails two days after a lead fills in a form, your automation can respond within 30 seconds, personalised, relevant, and ready to book a call. In almost every sector, the business that responds fastest and follows up most consistently wins more work. Automation makes that possible at scale.

Real World Examples

See exactly how automation works in businesses like yours.

5 minute read

Lead follow-up

The problem: A potential customer fills in a contact form on your website. Your sales team is busy. The lead sits in an inbox for two days. By the time someone emails back, the prospect has already spoken to a competitor.

The automation: The moment the form is submitted, the automation reads the enquiry using AI, drafts a personalised response based on what the person asked about, sends it from your email address, adds the contact to your CRM, and creates a follow-up task for your team. All of this happens in under 60 seconds, at any time of day.

Invoice processing

The problem: Invoices arrive by email from dozens of suppliers in different formats. Someone has to open each one, read the details, enter them into your accounting system, and file the email. It takes hours every week and is prone to typos.

The automation: Each incoming invoice email is automatically detected, the AI reads the attachment and extracts the supplier name, amount, date, and invoice number, and creates the corresponding record in your accounting software. The email is labelled and archived. No manual entry required.

Client onboarding

The problem: Every time you win a new client, someone has to manually send a welcome email, create a project folder, set up the client in your project management tool, send a contract, and schedule a kickoff call. Miss any step and it reflects poorly on your business.

The automation: When a contract is signed, the entire onboarding sequence fires automatically. Welcome email goes out. Project folder is created with the right structure. Client profile is set up in your tools. A kickoff booking link is sent. Every new client gets exactly the same smooth experience, every time.

Social media scheduling

The problem: Staying consistent on LinkedIn or Instagram takes significant time. Writing posts, finding images, scheduling them, and tracking what performs well is a job in itself.

The automation: You add a topic or a link to a spreadsheet. The AI drafts a post in your brand voice. It gets added to a scheduling queue and published at the optimal time. High-performing posts are flagged for you to review and repurpose.

Common Questions

Honest answers to the things people ask us most often.

7 minute read
Do I need to understand the technology to benefit from it?
Not at all. The whole point of working with an agency like ours is that you do not have to understand the underlying technology. You understand your business and your problems. We translate those into solutions. All we need from you is clarity on what is painful, repetitive, or slow in your day-to-day operations.
Is my business data safe?
Data security is something we take seriously at every step. We only use enterprise-grade AI providers who have strong data protection agreements in place and do not use your data to train their models. All automations are built using secure authentication and we never store sensitive data beyond what is needed to perform the task. We are happy to walk you through the security setup for your specific workflow before anything is built.
How long does it take to get set up?
Most automations are built and live within 3 to 5 working days from the point we have everything we need from you. Larger, more complex systems may take longer. We will always give you a clear timeline before we start work so there are no surprises. The free audit at the start of our process is specifically designed to scope everything accurately upfront.
What happens if something breaks or stops working?
Automations are monitored continuously. Most issues are caught and resolved before you notice anything. If something does go wrong on your end, you contact us and we fix it. This is included as part of ongoing maintenance for all clients. We also build every workflow with error handling built in, so if a step fails, you get a clear alert rather than silent data loss.
Can I make changes after the automation is built?
Yes. Businesses change, and your automations should change with them. Minor adjustments, like updating the wording of an email or adding a new step to a workflow, are handled quickly as part of your maintenance arrangement. For larger changes, we scope them as a small project and give you a clear timeline and cost before starting.
What tools and apps do you work with?
We work with the tools most small and medium businesses already use. Gmail, Google Workspace, HubSpot, Salesforce, Notion, Airtable, Slack, Xero, QuickBooks, Stripe, Shopify, WhatsApp, LinkedIn, and many more. If you use a tool and are unsure whether it can be automated, ask us and we will tell you honestly whether it is possible and what it would involve.
Will automation make some of my team redundant?
This is a very understandable concern and it is worth being direct about it. Automation removes tasks, not roles. The people who previously spent two hours a day on data entry now spend those two hours on higher-value work that only they can do. In practice, our clients typically find that their teams are happier after automation because the boring, repetitive parts of their jobs disappear.
How much does it cost?
Cost depends on the complexity and number of automations you need. The best place to start is with a free audit, where we map out your workflows and give you a clear proposal with honest pricing before you commit to anything. There is no obligation and nothing to lose. Book a call with us to get started.
What is the difference between AI and automation?
Automation is the plumbing: it connects your tools and moves information between them based on rules. AI is the intelligence layer: it reads, understands, and generates content as part of that workflow. On their own, automations are useful but limited. Combined with AI, they become capable of handling nuanced, language-based tasks that previously required a human. Most of what we build uses both together.

Something is not working

3 minute read
🙏

We know how frustrating it is when something that is supposed to save you time suddenly stops. We take that seriously. Please do not hesitate to reach out and we will treat it as a priority.

Before you contact us, try these quick checks

Most issues have a simple cause. Working through this list takes two minutes and may save you a call.

  • 1 Check that the apps your automation connects to are still working normally. You can check the live status of all the platforms we use on our status page. If a service is reporting an outage, the automation will resume automatically once it recovers.
  • 2 Check whether you have changed your password or revoked access to any of the connected apps recently. Automations use a secure connection token, and that token stops working if your credentials change.
  • 3 Check that the trigger you are expecting is actually firing. For example, if the automation starts when a form is submitted, submit a test entry and see whether that alone triggers the workflow.
  • 4 Check your email inbox and spam folder for any notifications from us. If we have detected an issue, we may have already reached out to you.

Common reasons an automation stops

💳
Credit or usage allowance has run out

Every automation platform has a monthly usage allowance. If your account has hit its limit for the month, workflows will pause until the allowance resets or is topped up.

What to do: Let us know and we will check the account balance immediately. If this is a recurring issue we can look at upgrading the plan or optimising the workflow to use fewer operations.
🔑
A connected app revoked its access

Occasionally a third-party app will expire or revoke the connection that the automation relies on, particularly after a password change, a security update, or a period of inactivity.

What to do: This is a quick fix on our end. Message us and we will reconnect the integration and run a test to make sure everything is flowing again.
🔧
The structure of your data changed

If a form, spreadsheet, or CRM field was renamed, removed, or restructured, the automation may no longer know where to find the information it needs. This can happen after a platform update or a team member making changes to a shared tool.

What to do: Think about whether anything was changed in the tools the automation connects to. Let us know what changed and we will update the workflow to match.
🌐
A third-party API changed

Software companies periodically update their APIs, which are the technical interfaces that automations use to communicate with their platforms. When an API changes without warning, workflows that relied on the old version can break.

What to do: This is entirely on us to fix. We monitor for API changes but occasionally one slips through. Contact us and we will get it resolved as a priority.
🤖
The AI model received an unexpected input

If your automation includes an AI step, it may occasionally receive an input that falls outside what it was designed to handle, for example an email in an unusual format or a response that is far longer than expected. This can cause the step to produce an error or an unhelpful output.

What to do: Note down roughly when it stopped and what type of input triggered it if you can. That helps us diagnose it much faster. Send us a message and we will investigate.
⏸️
The workflow was paused automatically

Most automation platforms will pause a workflow after a series of consecutive errors to prevent runaway failures. This is a protective measure, but it means the automation stops processing new requests until someone reviews it.

What to do: We should have been notified automatically when this happened. If you contact us, we will review the error log, fix the underlying issue, and resume the workflow.
